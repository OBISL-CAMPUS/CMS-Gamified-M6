PLC Chronological Pipeline

Sectors change as markets age. Products migrate across sequential sockets: Introduction, Growth, Maturity, and Decline. Sequence the blocks chronologically above to construct the correct strategy timeline.

Marketing Strategy Adapters

1. Introduction: High development cost, low volume, focus on Innovators.
2. Growth: Rapidly expanding sales volume, competitors enter, focus on Early Adopters.
3. Maturity: Pricing wars peak, market saturation occurs, focus on Early/Late Majority.
4. Decline: Commodity trap risk, margin erosion, requires deletion or continuous product rejuvenation.

Theory 3 of 4

Elasticity & Price Calibration

Understand Price Elasticity of Demand (Ed = %ΔQ / %ΔP). Pivot price controls above using the sliders. If demand is inelastic (|Ed| < 1), price increases boost revenue. If elastic (|Ed| > 1), price cuts capture volume. Tap active nodes and slide to hit target revenue!

Inelastic Pricing Formula

$$\text{Total Revenue (TR)} = \text{Price (P)} \times \text{Quantity (Q)}$$
When demand is highly inelastic, any percentage increase in price is much larger than the corresponding percentage decline in volume, driving up total cash flows.

Strategy Definitions
